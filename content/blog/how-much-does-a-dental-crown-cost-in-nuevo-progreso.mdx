## Types of Dental Crowns Explained

### Porcelain Fused to Metal (PFM)
PFM crowns combine the strength of metal with the appearance of porcelain. They're durable and relatively affordable, making them a popular choice. The main drawback is that over time, the metal can show at the gum line, creating a dark line that some patients find unesthetic.

### All-Porcelain/Ceramic Crowns
These crowns offer the best aesthetic results because they're made entirely of tooth-colored material. They look completely natural and are excellent for front teeth where appearance matters most. They're slightly weaker than PFM but still plenty durable for back teeth.

### Zirconia Crowns
Zirconia is the strongest crown material available. It's incredibly durable, resistant to chipping, and provides excellent aesthetics. Zirconia crowns are ideal for patients who grind their teeth or need maximum longevity.

### E-max (Pressed Ceramic)
E-max is a premium ceramic that combines the natural appearance of porcelain with enhanced durability. It's a popular choice for patients who want the best of both worlds.

## What to Expect During Your Dental Crown Procedure

The process is the same whether you're getting a crown in the US or Mexico:

1. **Consultation**: The dentist examines your tooth and takes X-rays to determine if a crown is necessary
2. **Tooth Preparation**: The tooth is shaped and prepared to receive the crown
3. **Impression**: A mold or digital scan is taken for the permanent crown
4. **Temporary Crown**: A temporary crown is placed to protect your tooth
5. **Lab Work**: Your permanent crown is fabricated
6. **Placement**: At your follow-up visit, the permanent crown is cemented in place

Most patients complete the procedure in two visits spaced 1-2 weeks apart. Some modern clinics in Nuevo Progreso offer same-day crowns using CAD/CAM technology, though this is less common.
