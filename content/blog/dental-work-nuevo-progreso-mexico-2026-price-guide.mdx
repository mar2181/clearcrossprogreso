## Why Is Dental Work So Much Cheaper in Mexico?

This is the number one concern people have — and it's a fair question. The lower prices aren't because of lower quality. Here's what's actually going on:

### 1. Lower Operating Costs
Rent, utilities, insurance, and staff salaries in Mexico are significantly lower than in the US. A dental office in Nuevo Progreso doesn't need to charge $1,500 per crown to keep the lights on.

### 2. No Insurance Overhead
US dental offices spend enormous amounts of time and money dealing with insurance companies — billing departments, claim denials, coding specialists. Mexican clinics operate on a direct-pay model, cutting out that entire layer of cost.

### 3. Education Costs
A Mexican dental degree costs a fraction of what an American dental degree costs. US dentists often graduate with $300,000–$500,000 in student loans. Mexican dentists don't carry that burden, so they don't need to pass it on to patients.

### 4. Competition
Nuevo Progreso has over **100 dental clinics** within a few blocks of the border. That level of competition keeps prices honest and quality high — clinics that deliver bad work don't survive.

## Hidden Costs to Watch For

The prices above cover the procedure itself, but smart patients budget for the full trip:

### Travel Costs (from the RGV)
- **Bridge toll:** $1.00 per person (pedestrian, round trip)
- **Parking:** $2–$5 for all-day parking on the US side
- **Gas:** Negligible if you're already in the Rio Grande Valley

### Additional Dental Costs
- **Consultation:** Many clinics offer free consultations; some charge $20–$30 (applied to treatment)
- **Panoramic X-Ray:** $30–$50 if you don't have recent ones
- **Temporary crowns:** Sometimes included, sometimes $20–$40 extra
- **Medications:** Antibiotics and pain meds from Mexican pharmacies — usually $5–$15

### What About Return Visits?
Some procedures require multiple visits:
- **Crowns:** Usually 2 visits (prep + placement), 5–7 days apart
- **Implants:** 2–3 visits over 3–6 months (placement, healing, crown)
- **Dentures:** 2–4 visits over 2–3 weeks
- **Root canals + crowns:** 2–3 visits

If you're coming from outside the RGV, factor in hotel costs ($50–$80/night in the area) and time off work for return trips.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need a passport to cross into Nuevo Progreso?
Yes, for re-entry into the US you need a valid US passport, passport card, or Enhanced Driver's License. A regular driver's license alone is **not sufficient** for border crossing.

### Is Nuevo Progreso safe for dental tourists?
Nuevo Progreso is one of the safer border towns for day visitors. The main tourist and dental zone is concentrated within a few blocks of the bridge. Stick to the main street, go during daylight hours, and you'll be fine. Thousands of Americans cross every week for dental work.

### Can I pay with a credit card?
Most reputable clinics accept credit cards, but some offer a 3–5% cash discount. ATMs are readily available. US dollars are accepted everywhere in Nuevo Progreso — you don't need to exchange currency.

### Will my US dental insurance cover work done in Mexico?
Some US dental insurance plans cover work done internationally, but most don't. However, even without insurance coverage, the savings in Mexico typically exceed what you'd pay out-of-pocket with insurance in the US. Check with your insurance provider for specifics.
